# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Category creators who generate AI brackets, share them, and return to see how the crowd voted.
Daily returners who check the day's ranking, vote, and compare their picks to the crowd.
Context: someone who nerds out on opinions and rankings — uses this to validate their taste or prove
they're ahead of the curve. Arrives curious, leaves either smug or intrigued.

## Product Purpose

Crowd-powered 1v1 bracket voting that answers "what does everyone actually think?" The core
mechanic is the reveal: you vote through a bracket, then see how your choices compare to the
aggregate crowd. AI generation removes friction for creators — any topic becomes a ranked list
within seconds. Daily rankings give casual users a reason to return. Success looks like: user votes,
sees the crowd split, shares the result or immediately starts another bracket.

## Brand Personality

Analytical nerd who likes to rank things and see what people think. Opinionated, data-transparent,
a little competitive. Not here to entertain — here to settle debates with numbers.

Three words: analytical, opinionated, communal.

## Anti-references

SaaS dashboards (corporate, cold, metric-obsessed for its own sake). Badge and streak mechanics
(gamification for engagement's sake, not insight). Anything that buries the data behind decoration.

## Design Principles

1. The reveal is the product. Every interaction builds toward the moment you see how the crowd
   voted. Don't interrupt that arc.
2. Numbers earn their place. Show real data — win rates, Elo, crowd splits. Trust the numbers
   to be interesting without dressing them up.
3. Strong choices only. Analytical nerds are opinionated; the design should be too. No hedging,
   no "safe" layouts, no mealy-mouthed copy.
4. Built for return, not just discovery. Prioritize the daily user's rhythm: fast to enter, fast
   to see results, fast to share.
5. Scale is accessibility. Big type and unambiguous affordances aren't a compromise — they are the
   standard. Every interactive element should be identifiable at a glance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prioritize large type and clearly identifiable interactive elements across all surfaces. Target
WCAG AA minimum. Smooth experience for broad audiences — no assumed context, no insider
terminology that needs explaining.
